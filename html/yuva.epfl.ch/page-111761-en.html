Ruthvika Shivani Ruthvika Shivani

Ruthvika Shivani

        

Profile

Date of Birth: 26th March, 1997
Coach : Pullela Gopichand
Training Base: Gopichand Badminton Academy, Hyderabad
Sport : Badminton

Achievements:

  • Ranked no. 10 in the World Junior Rankings; qualified for the 2014 Youth Olympics
  • Winner at the 2014 Gautam Thakkar Memorial All India Junior Badminton Championships in Mumbai
  • Winner at the 2013 Sushant Chipalkati Memorial India Junior International Badminton Championships in Pune
  • Singles winner in U-17 and U-19 categories at the 2012 All India Junior National Badminton Championships in Imphal
  • Quarter-finalist at the 2012 Asian Youth Badminton Championships in China


Ruthvika Shivani is a shy, quiet girl from Khammam in Andhra Pradesh. But on the badminton court, she has a reputation of blasting her way past her opponents. Her coach Pullela Gopichand feels that she has got all that it takes to reach the top. Tall and energetic, she has incredible court coverage and great control.

The Story

At the 2014 Asian Junior Badminton Championships in Malaysia, Ruthvika spearheaded the Indian attack, and went all the way till the last 8. Her stellar performances at the event and at preceding international competitions have helped Ruthvika amass points to be ranked in the top-10 in the world – India’s highest ranked junior player in Badminton.

By virtue of her international ranking, Ruthvika will now represent India at the 2014 Youth Olympics in Nanjing, China. Ruthvika is currently training at the Gopichand Academy in Hyderabad, preparing for a good show at the Games in August.

 

The Next Big Thing

From Prakash Padukone and Pullela Gopichand to Saina Nehwal, P Kashyap and PV Sindhu – you could say Indian badminton has had its share of world class players, especially in the last few years. Today, under Chief National Coach Pullela Gopichand’s expert guidance, the new breed of Indian shuttlers is challenging the best badminton players in the world. And to top it all, they’re winning a lot of medals for India!

Young Ruthvika has been performing consistently at the national circuit now for the last few years. Ranked in the national top 3 in both U-17 and U-19 categories, Ruthvika even competes in the senior women’s category, and consistently features in the top 10!

 

“I believe that I can win the gold medal at the 2014 Youth Olympics in Nanjing, China.” - Ruthvika
