          2009 2009

          2009

          23.11.09 The European Space Agency has published the list of Young Graduates Traineeships (YGT) which allows young engineers (just after graduation) to spend one year at ESA. Deadline to apply for most position is set to 13 December 2009. [More]
          28.10.09 In the frame of the ESA REXUS/BEXUS programme to take place in 2010/11, opportunities for flights on stratospheric balloons and sounding rockets for payloads developed by European students are offered. Note the call closes on 16 Nov. 2009. [More]
          24.09.09 Communications in both directions (uplink and downlink) have been successfully established with SwissCube. [More]
          23.09.09 SwissCube, the first Swiss satellite entirely designed by students has been successfully launched from India at 8h21.
          16.09.09 The launch of the satellite SwissCube has been confirmed for Wednesday 23 September 2009 at 8h16 (Swiss time) on board of an Indian PSLV rocket.
          08.09.09 A presentation about the Minor in Space Technologies will take place on Friday 18 September in auditorium ELA-1 at EPFL from 12h15 to 13h00 (copy of the presentation in PDF format available). The Minor is open to all new EPFL Master students.
          21.07.09 The satellite SwissCube has successfully completed its test phase and has been shipped to India for a launch to take place in the second half of the summer 2009. [More]
          15.07.09 The second issue of the Space Center EPFL Newsletter has been published.
          13.07.09 The 2008 Annual report of the Space Center EPFL is now available. [More]
          06.07.09 To celebrate the 40th anniversary of the moon landing, the Space Center EPFL will organize the 4th EPFL Space Research Day on the topic: " 40 years in space: From the moon landings to today's space missions". The event will take on Friday 25 September 2009 from 13h30 onwards at the EPFL Polydôme.
          22.06.09 As founding member of the Center since 2003, RUAG Aerospace recently renewed its special partnership with the Space Center EPFL for the period 2009-2012.
          15.06.09 All the handouts and vugraphs presented on 9 June 2009 during the training course on "Space Radiation and its effect on EEE Components" are available in PDF format.
          08.05.09 The list of student projects (both semester and Master) for the Autumn 2009 offered by the Space Center EPFL is now available. [More]
          07.05.09 The Space Center EPFL has an opportunity for a PhD student in the frame of the HyperSwissNet project related to optical and microwave remote sensing.  [More]
          23.04.09 ESA's Education Office is offering European students to fly their experiments in microgravity by participating in a series of parabolic flights on the Airbus A300 Zero-G aircraft. Deadline to apply is set to 22 June 2009. [More]
          22.04.09 Up to 8 EPFL students will be able to participate in the BMSTU Space Camp from 4 to 14 July 2009 in Moscow. [More]
          17.04.09 Together with ESA, the Space Center EPFL will organise on Tuesday 9 June 2009 at EPFL a training course entitled "Space Radiation and its effect on EEE Components". [More]
          16.04.09 The "Centre Suisse d'Electronique et de Microtechnique (CSEM)" renewed its special partnership with the Space Center EPFL for the period 2009-2012
          15.04.09 The Foundation « Jean Monnet pour l'Europe » linked to the university of Lausanne will organise a seminar (in French) on the "Participation de la Suisse à la collaboration spatiale européenne". The workshop will take place on Monday 27 April 2009 from 15h15 to 17h00 in the room 2013 at the Anthropole building of the University of Lausanne. Contact gilles.grin@fjme.unil.ch, tel 021 692 2090 for more information.
          06.03.09 A delegation of the Bauman Moscow State Technical University (BMSTU) composed of 9 students and professors visited EPFL during 3 days to discuss possible cooperation projects in the space domain. This visit followed the highly successful stage of EPFL students in July 2008 in the frame of the BMSTU Space Camp. [More]
          16.02.09 In the frame of the Minor in Space Technologies, Professor Claude Nicollier teaches the class “Technologies et opérations spatiales”, a very popular lecture which is for the first time also video-transmitted life to the ETHZ in Zürich.
          09.01.09 The second issue of the SwissCube Newsletter has been published. [More]
