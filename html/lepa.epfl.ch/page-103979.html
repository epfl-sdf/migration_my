    Swiss Dual Circuit Redox Flow Battery - Demonstrator for Energy Storage and Hydrogen Production

    RFB Demonstration project is part of the SCCER Storage

     

    The Swiss Competence Center for Energy Research “Heat and Electricity Storage” (SCCER Storage), is a virtual center composed of Swiss Universities and Universities of Applied Sciences. The center was established by the Swiss Government and is situated at the Paul Scherrer Institute (PSI) since January 2014. The head of the SCCER Storage is Prof. Dr. Thomas Justus Schmidt, who also leads the Laboratory for Electrochemistry at PSI.

    SCCER Storage is a major funding body of the project from January 2014-December 2016.

     

     

     

     

     

    A Dual-Circuit Redox Flow Battery Demonstrator

    In November 2013 LEPA received funding from the Swiss Federal Office of Energy (SFOE) and other project partners to scale-up a dual-circuit redox flow battery to the demonstrator level. Our objective is to adapt a commerical 10 kW redox flow battery with secondary circuits which we will then operate and optimise to generate hydrogen alongside storing electrochemical energy. This approach is designed to complement electrochemical energy storage so as to circumvent the low energy denisty of the RFBs.

    This site will document, update and inform interested members of the public as to our progress with this challenging yet exciting scale-up, as it proceeds.
