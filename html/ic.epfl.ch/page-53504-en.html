2003 Edition

Systems-on-Chip

Methodological Challenges and Advanced Multimedia Applications

The evolution of silicon manufacturing capabilities makes it possible to produce full-fledged computing systems on a single chip. Such compact, inexpensive and extremely powerful computing engines have already enabled to run complex multimedia applications in new everyday objects (eg cellular phones, digital cameras, watches) and to develop equipment of previously unheard-of sophistication (e.g., networking equipment, high-speed modems, color printers, digital radios).
Nothing seems to prevent this evolution to continue for several more years, with systems with more than one billion transistors soon to be available and ready to accommodate new applications and algorithms. How to design quickly and effectively such extremely complex systems poses unprecedented challenges.

The EPFL School of Computer and Communication Sciences is pleased to invite you to its Annual Research Day 2002, entitled

Global Computing

A look into challenging research in computing and information technologies, that could change our everyday life!

Global computing refers to programming environments and information systems where the underlying assumption is that there is not a single resource provider. Resources may be humans, organizations, services, knowledge and information, computer programs, and machines. The challenge is to be able to define and build systems which harmoniously work and cooperate in such a multi-faceted framework.

The Communication Systems Department (DSC) of EPFL is pleased to invite you to its fourth Annual Research Day, which is dedicated to

Mobile Information and Communication Systems (MICS)

Systems that can communicate everywhere do not necessarily need to rely on centralized resources to provide useful services. Such decentralized systems require the ability to be self-organized. These "infrastructure-less" networks of mobile information devices will find many applications, from instant networking abilities in professional environments to bootstrapping communications in disaster areas.
In collaboration with several Swiss institutions, our department has prepared a proposal for a National Competence Center in Research around self-organized Mobile Information and Communication Systems. The presentations will cover selected aspects of this subject, from fundamental questions through networking issues to applications.

1999 Edition

The Communication Systems Division (SSC) of EPFL is pleased to invite you to its third Annual Research Day, which is dedicated to

Mobile Communication Systems

It is projected that, in the 21st century, it will be possible to establish wireless links of tens of megabits per second between any two points on the globe. Achieving this goal requires making more efficient use of the networks and of the boundary conditions such as available bandwidth, transmit power and antenna volume. Advances in digital communications, coding and information theory make it now possible to address these issues. Solutions will also depend on the availability of new, scalable application and service concepts.
The goal of the morning talks will be to give a picture of the present situation, the main issues, the problems to be solved, the possible directions for their solution and a vision into the future.

Technical visits (descriptions)

Communication Networks Laboratory
(LRC, INR113, Prof. Le Boudec)
Research at LRC focuses on the internal mechanisms of communication networks. We shall show results and prototypes of AREQUIPA, a method for combining ATM and TCP/IP in a simple and effective way. The method is implemented on LINUX and is becoming part of the standard distribution. We will show our work on improving the performance of the Web with ATM, for applications such as Web caching or Teleteaching. We shall also show results on the performance of TCP/IP when it is used over ATM or over satellite links. Network control methods and theories are key for reducing communication costs in services with reservations. We shall show our ongoing researches using network calculus. Optical networks pose hard challenges for survivability and fault management; we shall show two methods for improving design and alarm filtering.

Computer Networking Laboratory
(LTI, INN326, Prof. Petitpierre)
The Computer Networking Laboratory will present a new approach to the development of interactive, concurrent, and distributed software. This approach complements Object Oriented Programming by providing a precise framework for the implementation phase. This framework supports the implementation of the models derived during the analysis phase, and contains tools that can be used to test, debug and validate the resulting applications. It is embedded in the same mathematical theories as specialized environments like SDL, Estelle or LOTOS, while offering a full access to all the libraries provided by the underlying operating systems. It can be used with very little extra learning for the heuristic verification of protocols and communicating systems.

Operating Systems Laboratory
(LSE, INF233, Prof. Schiper)
The Operating Systems Laboratory research is devoted to fault-tolerance in distributed systems. In this context we are currently implementing various plateforms to support the development of distributed replicated services. This includes the development of a CORBA replication service. Assistants will be available to demonstrate and comment the latest developments.

Computer Graphics Laboratory
(LIG, INJ118, Prof. Thalmann)
Computer Graphics Lab will show demos about Virtual Reality, Shared Virtual Environments, and Virtual Humans. In particular, we shall demonstrate the VLNET system which allows distant partners to share a common 3D environment, play games, cooperate, discuss, or manipulate objects. Simulation of virtual humans will also be shown, as well as applications involving sensors, CyberGloves and Head-mounted display.

Industrial Computer Engineering Laboratory
(LIT, INN115, Prof. Wegmann)
The LIT (Laboratoire d'Informatique Technique) started new activities at the beginning of 1997. Its mission is to teach and research in the field of distributed information systems applied to industrial applications. Currently the main laboratory activities are related to the development of new courses using a "case-base" teaching method. These courses cover industrial information systems (embedded systems, fieldbuses, plant networks, ...) as well as commercial ones (business engineering, SAP, Intranet, etc.). The research activities relate to two main fields:

  • tele-maintenance and embedded system management: the goal is to deploy communication and information systems technologies in embedded system management. This will allow to do remote maintenance and quality management  of large embedded systems (e.g. buildings, trains,  power stations, etc.) via Internet and the embedded application network (fieldbus). The technologies developped will become instrumental for vendors of embedded systems (e.g. ABB, Landis & Gyr, etc.), as their customers will tend to outsource more and more the management of their assets.
  • collaborative computing : the purpose is to improve and boost the "collective IQ" of an enterprise by capturing corporate knowledge and deploying tools to improve collaborative work. One of the applications of these concepts is the Web site named "Oeuf" (Observatoire d'Etude de l'Université du Futur), the goal of which is to become a site where people collaborate to capture trends in the human knowledge evolution. Other activities are started more specifically on capturing technical knowledge. These technologies will also be used to manage the laboratory expertise and know-how.

 

Electronics Laboratory
(LEG, ELB328, Prof. Declercq and Enz)
The research activities are covering all the aspects related to the design of integrated circuits, including technology, circuit design, system architectures and CAD. The LEG has gained an international reputation for the results achieved in the field of low-power electronics. In the more recent years some new activities have emerged. Particularly, the development of RF circuits for low-power wireless communication systems.
In addition, the LEG has a long tradition of industrial and scientific collaboration, both national and international. Numerous research contracts exist with various Swiss organisations, in particular the National Science Foundation, CTI and MicroSwiss. European Research Programmes such as ESPRIT, EUREKA and JESSI support an important part of the LEG's research, adding a European scope to its activities.
The visit is organized as follows: Introduction to the design of integrated circuits: from the idea to the silicon; presentation of several research projects with an emphasis on low-power wireless communication systems; visit of the IC lab.

Chair of Circuits and Systems
(CIRC, ELE115, Prof. Hasler)
The Chair of Circuits and Systems will present its research centered on nonlinear circuits and systems. In particular, the following research topics will be presented by demos and posters:

  • Transmission of information using a chaotic carrier signal. A demo developed in a student project will illustrate the concept.
  • Parameter identification in nonlinear systems. A demo will show the identification of the parameters of a nonlinear system that may have chaotic behavior, using synchronization.
  • Modeling of superconductors with high critical temperature (liquid nitrogen temperature). A demo will show how the AC-losses in a superconducting strip are measured.
  • Speech recognition. A demo developed in a student project will show the voice command of a small robot.

 

Telecommunication Systems Lab
(TCOM-TNG, ELD324, Prof. Fontolliet) (TCOM-SG, ELE228, Prof. Hubaux)
TCOM-TNG (Transmission systems and Networks Group) is participating with the following topics (posters & demos):

  • Modelling and experimental validation of ATM traffic control and network resource management policies related to various service classes (CBR, VBR, ABR and UBR). Work performed within the Europen project ACTS 094 EXPERT.
  • System implications of fibre grating devices in high-speed fibre optics transmission systems. Chromatic dispersion compensation using fibre Bragg gratings: modelling and performance evaluation. Work performed within the European project ACTS 046 PHOTOS (PHOtosensitives Technologies for Optical Systems).
TCOM-TSG (Telecommunication Services Group) carries out several research projects focused both on the creation and validation of services and on the provision of the appropriate level of quality of service for multimedia communication. Following projects will be presented: OAMS, a TINA-compliant environment running on CORBA; creation and validation of telecommunication services running on CORBA; management of multimedia resources; remote inspection of medical images over high speed networks; transmission of MPEG streams over ATM (Network adaptation, AAL, Application of psychovisual techniques); reliability and security of distributed platforms.

 

Signal Processing Laboratory
(LTS-VG, ELD225, Prof. Kunt) (LTS-TP, ELD243, Prof. de Coulon)
The Speech Processing Group (SPG, Prof. de Coulon) of LTS proposes to visit its Speech Processing Laboratory, and to present the Interactive Voice Server (IVS) prototype demonstrators developed in the framework of the CTI project "Reconnaissance automatique de la parole en français sur station de travail raccordée à SwissNet". The goal of this ongoing project is to make available basic technologies for automatic speech recognition and speaker verification on multi-processor SunSPARC workstation and SwissNet (ISDN) platform to industrial partners and particularly to Swiss industry for Swiss French. The research addresses among others the problem of phonetic models for telephone quality Swiss French. The approach selected for this project to model command words is the speaker independent flexible vocabulary approach. It offers the potential to build word models for any speaker using Swiss French and for any application vocabulary from a single set of trained phonetic sub-word units. The developed algorithms provide necessary tools to design and implement workstation oriented voice messaging and voice response demonstrators.
In the areas of Videocoding, Computervision and Biomedical Signal Processing (Prof. Kunt), the following subjects will be demonstrated: 3D and 4D modeling of the heart from MRI sequences; compression of echocardiographic image sequences; Analysis of skin lesion images; DNA shape reconstruction from image pairs; Person authentication with face images; Time-frequency analysis of heart rate signals; Source separation techniques for cardiovascular signals; Synthetic natural hybrid coding; View-dependent scalability for virtual reality applications; LTS contribution to MPEG-4.

Electromagnetics and Acoustics Lab
(LEMA, ELB039, Prof. Gardiol, Mosig, Rossi and Skrivervik)
The research at the Laboratory of Electromagnetics and Acoustics has several centers of interest. Presentations and demos will be made on the following subjects: millimeter-wave and submillimeter-wave (30GHz-3THz) antennas and antenna systems, where the front end of the receiver is directly integrated in the antenna; antenna miniaturisation for mobile-communication applications; developpement of theoretical models and numerical tools for the design of micro- and millimeter-wave structures; acoustics and communication.

Metrology Laboratory
(MET, ELG231, Prof. Robert)
Ultra high capacity FDM optical local area network:
Experimental demonstration of a broadband multiple access coherent lightwave communication Local Area Network (LAN) in a star topology will be shown. This experimental network is under development in order to reach the following characteristics:
(1) high number of nodes (> 100), (2) multi-wavelength system (optical FDM) corresponding to different communication channels, (3) high total capacity (multi-Gbits/s) together with moderate capacity of each channel (to date 140 Mb/s).
One main aspect of this project is to evaluate theoretically and experimentally the new possibilities brought by the coherent communication systems for increasing the transmission capacity of a Local Area Network by dense frequency division multiplexing and also for increasing the inter-nodes distance of the LAN without repeater or amplifier.

Audio-visual Communication Laboratory
(LCAV, ELD010, Prof. Vetterli)
The focus of this newly established laboratory is signal processing theory and its applications in communications systems. In particular, the  following topics will be presented:; wavelet theory and applications, in particular multiwavelets; universal compression algorithms, in particular using optimal segmentation; interaction of source and channel coding with multiresolution source coders; multiresolution graphic and image representation; automatic indexing of image databases; signal processing teaching support using java.
