Welcome to the EPSL website

The Earth and Planetary Science Laboratory belongs to the Institute of Physics (IPHYS) of the EPFL. It works to apply the techniques of physics and chemistry to the behavior of Earth and planetary materials in order to understand processes of planetary surfaces, mantles and cores.

The Laboratory research focus spans many fields, some examples are given below.

Clathrate hydrates under planetary conditions

  • Fast methane diffusion at the interface of two clathrate structures

    by Umbertoluca Ranieri et al.

    Nature Communications, 2017

Physical properties of mantle materials

  • Spin and valence dependence of iron partitioning in Earth’s deep mantle

    by Hélène Piet et al.

    PNAS, 2016

  • Effects of Fe-enrichment on the equation of state and stability of (Mg,Fe)SiO3 perovskite

    by Susannah M. Dorfman et al.

    Earth and Planetary Science Letters, 2013

Planetary ices

  • Ice VII from aqueous salt solutions: From a glass to a crystal with broken H-bonds

    by Stefan Klotz, Livia E. Bove et al.

    Nature - Scientific Reports, 2016

  • Effect of salt on the H-bond symmetrization in ice

    by Livia E. Bove et al.

    PNAS, 2015

Earth’s core composition

  • An early geodynamo driven by exsolution of mantle components from Earth’s core

    by James Badro et al.

    Nature, 2016

  • Core formation and core composition from coupled geochemical and geophysical constraints

    by James Badro et al.

    PNAS, 2015

Remote sensing of planetary bodies

  • A deep crust–mantle boundary in the asteroid 4 Vesta

    by Harold Clenet et al.

    Nature, 2014

Shock processes in meteorites

  • Carbon polymorphism in shocked meteorites: Evidence for new natural ultrahard phases

    by Tristan Ferroir, Philippe Gillet et al.

    Earth and Planetary Science Letters, 2010

  • Timescales of shock processes in chondritic and martian meteorites

    by Pierre Beck, Philippe Gillet et al.

    Nature, 2005

Plate tectonics

  • Creep of phyllosilicates at the onset of plate tectonics

    by Elodie Amiguet et al.

    Earth and Planetary Science Letters, 2012
