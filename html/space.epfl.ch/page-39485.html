          2005

          12.2005 The deadline for the 2006 Parabolic Flight Campaign has been extended to January 15th, 2006. More information can be found on our student flights web page.
          12.2005 Interested in spending a week in Austria from 25 July to 3 August 2006 to "Monitor Natural Hazards from Space"? Then join the Alpbach Summer School 2006 . Get more information here or write to space.center@epfl.ch.
          11.2005 A presentation of the main results obtained during the 2005 ESA student parabolic flight campaign will be presented by the two participating EPFL teams (Colibri, Flash & Splash) on Monday 7 November 2005 at 10h30 in the EPFL Polydôme.
          9.2005 Prof. Mosig and his group at EPFL have developed a new generation of space hardware that could function as both antenna and solar cell array. A true test of this innovative concept will take place at the end of October 2005, when a German satellite will be launched with the equipment on board. See our press release.
          8.2005 In collaboration with the Press and Information Office of the EPFL, the Space Center organized a press seminar about space activities at EPFL for a delegation from North America. The presentations are available for download.
          8.2005 Download stunning images of the Space Shuttle above Switzerland taken from onboard the International Space Station during the flip manoeuvres in the last mission, at an altitude of 353 km.
          8.2005 Space Center EPFL launches an improved web site, offering more information on education, research and business in space. Upcoming events and opportunities on campus and around Europe are posted on our redesigned front page; other new features are to appear in the near future.
          7.2005 ESA released a Call For Proposals to enhance cooperation and facilitate research partnerships with universities and academic research groups active, or planning to be active, in the space sector.
          7.2005 Two EPFL student teams just completed their zero-gravity experiments during the ESA parabolic flight campaign with great success. The Colibri Team built a free-flyer (30x30x30 cm) with 6 degrees of freedom, able to intelligently move in a 0g environment while the Flash & Splash Team studied cavity collapse at spherical free surfaces in microgravity.
          5.2005 NASA, in collaboration with the Florida Space Research Institute (FSRI), recently announced a new Centennial Challenges prize competition. The MoonROx (Moon Regolith Oxygen) Challenge will award $250,000 to the first team that can extract breathable oxygen from simulated lunar soil before the prize expires on 1 June 2008.
          5.2005 The Space Center EPFL is looking for an outstanding scientific collaborator to be part of a team promoting and developing space applications at EPFL.
          4.2005 "Career Opportunities at the European Space Agency" that took place on Friday 22 April 2005 at the EPFL Polydome was a full success with more than 200 participants.
          4.2005 Contraves Space, largest in the Swiss space industry, has decided to solidify its partnership with EPFL. The Zurich-based company, part of the Unaxis group, signed a three-year collaborative agreement with the Space Center [PDF]
          3.2005 Two 4-month placements are offered by ESA to young graduate engineers to work on a project to study Lunar Surface Mobility. Please contact us if interested.
          3.2005 An EPFL student team has been selected to fly a zero-gravity experiment during the next ESA parabolic flight campaign in July 2005.
          2.2005

          Space activities at EPFL, ESA, as well as industrial partners, were presented in a meeting for all professors. See Seminar Agenda.
