          Call for Ideas 2015


          “To identify and demonstrate disruptive space innovations based on ideas and concepts”


          The Swiss Space Office of the State Secretariat for Education, Research and Innovation of the Swiss Confederation (SERI/SSO) is initiating the second Call for Ideas to foster low Technology Readiness Level (typically TRL 1-2) research and development studies related to space activities. Issued for the second time after 2013, the objective of this Call is to identify and demonstrate disruptive space innovations based on ideas or concepts. Thanks to a short-term implementation and a relatively light administrative element, the selected studies shall focus on technical and scientific work. The SERI/SSO has entrusted the Swiss Space Center (SSC) to implement this Call for Ideas. The SSC will provide technical support to the studies as well as networking contacts with the aim to spur on ideas towards innovative products, developing niche sectors in Switzerland.

          Objectives

          The main objectives of this Call for Ideas are to foster and promote Swiss technological and scientific competences that have a clear potential for space products and services/applications. More particularly, this Call for Ideas aims:

          • to foster the development of novel ideas and disruptive concepts related to the space sector;
          • to trigger the interest of new entities, encouraging them to enter the space market and evaluate the potential of their proposed technology;
          • to better position Swiss academia and industry with regard to future European and global activities and to support the development of novel technologies and services for future space science and technology applications.

          Both the development of new space technologies and of new services related to space may be proposed.


          Topics

          For this Call for Ideas, the Swiss Space Center invites submissions on the following topics:

          • Micro-Nano Technologies (MNT)
          • Materials, Parts and Processes (MPP)
          • Pico- and Nano-Satellite technology and components
          • Novel technologies for scientific instruments


          These focal topics are based on the Swiss Space Implementation Plan (Federal Department of Economic Affairs, Education and Research, 2013) and the Strategic Plan of the Swiss Space Center issued by the strategic committee of the Swiss Space Center, which is composed by its academic and industrial members.


          Who may apply

          The present “Call for Ideas” is open to all Swiss actors. In light of the above-mentioned objectives of this Call, first consideration may be given to Universities, Swiss Federal Institutes of Technology (EPFL / ETH Zürich), Universities of Applied Sciences (HES/FH) and SME's (Small and medium-sized Enterprises) which propose novel technologies or technology spin-ins from non-space application domains.


          Format of the proposal

          1. Cover letter
          2. Technical & administrative proposal (10 pages max.)

          The technical & administrative proposal shall be structured according to the following main sections:

          • Technical and/or scientific objectives
          • Description of the study
          • Key personnel
          • Financial aspects
          • Payment plan (in principle 20% at kick-off, and 80% after reception of the final report)

          Please note that detailed costing based on ESA PSS standard is not required.


          Duration of the study and meetings/reviews 

          The proposed studies shall last for a maximum of 6 months from the kick-off to the delivery of the final report.

          The following reviews are planned at contractor premises or by teleconference:

          • Kick-Off meeting (date T0)
          • Progress Meeting (date T0 + 3 months)
          • Final Presentation (date T0 + 6 months)

           

          Deliverables

          A Final Report of the activity shall be provided to the Swiss Space Center at the end of the study, together with the presentations given during the reviews/meetings. The Final Report shall provide a complete description of all the work done during the activity and shall be self-standing. It shall cover the whole scope of the activity, i.e. a comprehensive introduction of the context, a description of the programme of work and report on the activities performed as well as the main results achieved. The deliverables shall be provided in electronic format (PDF).


          Financial support

          A maximum amount of 20’000 CHF is allocated to each selected study, to be considered as a unique subsidy which cannot be extended. The funding shall be used exclusively to pay salaries. No management or administrative costs are foreseen under this scheme. There is no direct follow-up funding foreseen in the frame of this Call for Proposals.

          The financial support being a public subsidy, the Value-Added Tax (VAT) does not apply (Swiss law on the VAT: MWSTG/LTVA/LIVA Art. 18).


          Intellectual property rights and confidentiality

          Intellectual property created in the study will remain with the proposer. In case the study is conducted by a consortium, a clear IP agreement will be required before the kick-off meeting.

          No information about the funded projects will be publicly available, unless prior written consent is obtained from the proposer(s).


          Evaluation procedure

          The Swiss Space Center will perform the technical and scientific evaluation by setting up an internal evaluation board. If needed, the evaluation board may contact external experts for recommendations. The final decision will be made by the Swiss Space Office.

           

          Schedule:

          • 16 March 2015: Publication of the “Call for Ideas”
          • 17 April 2015: Deadline to submit the proposals to the Swiss Space Center
          • 15 May 2015: All participants informed of the outcome of their submissions by the Swiss Space Center
          • June 2015: Kick-off meetings / Start of studies
          • 30 November 2015: End of studies, final report submitted


          Submission of the proposals

          Only proposals received by Friday 17 April 2015 at midnight will be accepted for evaluation. Proposals, written in English, shall be submitted in electronic format (PDF) to the following address:


          Swiss Space Center
          Tobias Bandi
          Station 13, EPFL
          CH-1015 Lausanne
          Tel: +41 (0)21 693 52 01
          Email: tobias.bandi@epfl.ch


          Questions and requests for clarifications

          Any questions related to this Call for Ideas shall be submitted to Tobias Bandi by email. All applicable information will be published at: http://space.epfl.ch/page-117426-en.html

           

          Q & A

          Question 1: Does a specific template document need to be used for the proposal?

          Answer 1: No. However, the format of the proposal shall comply with the requirements stated in the call.

          Question 2: Is it necessary to clearly state with is the topic of the proposal?

          Answer 2: A link of the proposal to one of the proposed topics is desired. Please note that both the development of new space technologies and of new services related to space may be proposed.
