          History

          Working for space projects has  been a tradition at EPFL since the early 80's. Some of the key domains of expertise developed in Lausanne at that time included aerodynamics, antennae and robotics for planetary exploration.

          In 2002, EPFL's  interest  in the space domain was confirmed by including space research in the 2004-2007 strategic plan. Under Professor Roland Siegwart's guidance, the Space Center EPFL was formally created in June 2003 when RUAG Aerospace decided to work together with EPFL in this domain.

          At the same time, Swiss astronaut and EPFL Professor, Claude Nicollier, started giving a very popular lecture on space technology attended by more than 150 students in 2004. The Microsystems for Space Technologies Laboratory (LMTS), chaired by Professor Herbert Shea, was also set-up at EPFL. 

          Rapidly key players in the Swiss space domain understood the advantage of taking part in the Space Center. The Swiss Space Office and Oerlikon Space AG (now RUAG Space) became members respectively in 2004 and 2005. The Centre Suisse d'Electronique et de Microtechnique (CSEM SA) and University of Neuchâtel joined in 2006, and Fachhochschule Nordwestschweiz followed suite in 2007.

          Faced with the constant growth of its' members, and with the arrival of Volker Gass as Director, it became necessary to change the structure of the Center, which was renamed Swiss Space Center in 2011, thus becoming a truly national entity.

          In  2013, the Board of Directors decided to seperate national and project activities. This process started in 2014, with the creation of the EPFL Space Center (eSpace), and a gradual transfer of projects to this new entity. The separation is ongoing and will be completed in 2016.
