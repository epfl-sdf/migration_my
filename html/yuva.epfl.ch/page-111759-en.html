Supriya Mondal

 

Supriya's Story  

Profile 

Date of Birth: 12/01/1997

Sport: Swimming

Training Base: Dolphin Academy, Bangalore Event(s): Butterfly, Individual Medley

Seventeen-year-old Supriya Mondal has been creating waves in the junior and senior national aquatic meets. He hails from a small fishing village near Kolkata, and his father – a fisherman – runs the household with a very modest income of about INR 5000 a month. Raw talent is what drives this young swimmer, and he has set the pool ablaze in the 50m, 100m and 200m butterfly events. He has won several medals in the 100m and 200m butterfly events and in the 400m Individual Medley, displaying great technique, skill and versatility. He even holds the National record for the 100m Butterfly event at the Junior level.

In 2012, GoSports Foundation selected Supriya for the PACE Scholarships. GSF helped Supriya relocate to Bangalore to train under Mr. Nihar Ameen, a National coach of high repute (who is also the coach of National champions and Olympic swimmers Sandeep Sejwal and Virdhawal Khade).

 

Achievements:

  • Qualified for the Youth Olympics in Nanjing, China, scheduled to be held in August 2014
  • Won 5 gold medals at the 2014 Junior National Swimming Championships in Bhopal
  • Won 2 gold and 1 silver medals at the 1st Dubai International Aquatic Championships
  • 1 silver and 1 bronze medal at the 2013 Senior National Swimming Championships in Trivandrum
  • Gold medal and national record in the 200m butterfly event and silver medal in the 100m butterfly event at the Junior National Championships in 2013
  • Represented India at the 2013 Asian Youth Games in Nanjing, China, and finished 6th in the 200 butterfly event
  • Won a gold medal in the 100m butterfly event and a silver medal in the 200m butterfly event at the Junior National Championships in 2012
  • Won gold medals in the 100m and 200m butterfly events, and a silver medal in the 50m butterfly event at the Junior National Championships in 2011 

 

The Olympic Dream

A shy young boy dreamt of representing India at the Olympics. His sheer determination and hard-work have taken him from a local tank in Bengal, to the 2014 Youth Olympics to China.

In April 2014, Supriya Mondal achieved the ‘A’ qualification timing in the 200m butterfly event, and qualified for the 2014 Youth Olympics. By doing so, he became the first Indian swimmer to seal a berth at the sporting extravaganza, which is scheduled to be held this August , in China.

 

The Story

Supriya is the son of a fisherman and hails from a small village called Shahpur near Kolkata in West Bengal. He first learned to swim in the village tank near his house, and very soon, he began winning multiple medals at all national competitions.

GoSports Foundation recognised his raw talent and incredible potential and has been supporting Supriya’s journey as part of its scholarships programme. In 2012 GoSports Foundation relocated Supriya to Bangalore, and has been taking care of Supriya’s living and training expenses in Bangalore, as well as his competition expenses across India and abroad.

“My aim is to win a medal for India at the 2014 Youth Olympics in Nanjing, China" — Supriya Mondal
