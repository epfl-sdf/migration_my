Stanford Neurosciences Institute, USA

EPFL-Stanford Exchange Program-funded by Firmenich

EPFL has partnered with two of Stanford University's new interdisciplinary bioscience institutes: Stanford ChEM-H (Chemistry, Engineering and Medicine for Human Health) and Stanford Neurosciences Institute to develop an exchange program and foster meaningful long-term collaborations between researchers at Stanford and EPFL in a variety of life science fields, with a particular emphasis on chemical biology and neuroscience.

 

The program is generously funded by Firmenich, the world’s largest privately-owned company in the fragrance and flavor business.

Funding
Applicants selected by the program will be reimbursed for their travel expenses up to $11,000 USD or 11,000 CHF for a three month exchange. This award will cover the following cost categories: economy airfare, ground transportation, lodging, insurance fees and required university fees. Funding will be provided as a travel reimbursement. The exchange visit must be completed within 12 months of the notice of award.

Eligibility
Faculty, Postdocs and PhD students of all levels who are in good standing at either EPFL or Stanford University are eligible to apply. Applicants will be required to provide a brief proposal, CV, and letters of support from their PI and intended exchange host. Proposals from all areas of life science research will be considered, with some preference for projects in the areas of chemical biology and neuroscience (especially related to sensory neuroscience).

Speakers hosted by the BMI

Luis de Lecea, Department Psychiatry and Behavioral Sciences, Stanford University School of Medicine
Stanford, USA, June 21, 2017.

To Apply
All applicants must apply through the Stanford ChEM-H application portal.

BMI & Partners Annual PhD Students Neuroscience Symposium

As part of its effort to promote and enhance collaboration with its strategic partners, the BMI is organizing an annual PhD students’ symposium that will bring together PhD students from the BMI and its strategic partnering institutions. The symposium aims to provide the students with a forum to share their work, exchange ideas, explore collaborations, expand their network and discuss career and scientific topics of mutual interest.
