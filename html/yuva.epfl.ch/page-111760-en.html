Abhishek Yadav Abhishek Yadav

Abhishek Yadav

       

Profile 

Date of Birth : 9th July, 1996

Coach : Liu Zhong Fang

Training base : PSPB Academy, Ajmer

Sport : Table Tennis

Achievements :

  • Qualified for the 2014 Youth Olympics
  • Bronze medal at the 2013 Asian Youth Games in Nanjing, China
  • Gold medal at the 2013 Junior National Table Tennis Championships in New Delhi
  • Gold medals in singles, doubles and team event in U-18 category at the 2012 South Asian Championships in Shillong

Hailing from Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh, 18-year-old Abhishek Yadav is regarded by India’s top Table Tennis coaches as the “Future of Table Tennis in India”. The fiery south-paw packs a punch in each of his rallies, and has grown into a mature athlete over the past few years

 

Humble Beginnings

The beginnings, like most Indian athletes, were modest for Abhishek. His father, who runs a small bookstall, is the sole earning member in a family of five. Abhishek’s elder brother and sister introduced him to the sport when he was just three years old, and almost immediately, he demonstrated exceptional natural skill and ability. His first coach, Late Ms. Indu Mandra, convinced his father to allow him to take up the sport seriously. And there has been no looking back ever since.

 

The Olympic Dream

Already an integral part of the Indian Junior Team, Abhishek has won numerous medals in both national and international competitions. At the Asian Qualification Tournament in Bangkok in April 2014, Abhishek beat Singapore's Yin Jing Yuan 4-3 which helped him secure a spot to represent India at the 2014 Youth Olympics in Nanjing, China.

 

The Challenge

Abhishek’s talent was spotted by GoSports Foundation in 2012, and after tracking his performance over the year, he is now part of GoSports Foundation’s Athlete Scholarship programme. Abhishek plans to clinch a medal at the Youth Olympics, and further participate in a number of international events in the 2014-15 seasons. GoSports Foundation will support his journey through monetary grant and by giving him access to the best experts in all allied sports sciences.

For his table tennis equipment, protein and recovery supplements, Abhishek spends close to Rs. 2,50,000 lakh every year. Being one of the top junior players in the world, he needs constant support in order to improve, and reach the next level.

He is currently training at the PSPB Academy in Ajmer, preparing to face-off against some of the world’s best juniors at the 2014 Youth Olympics in China. He is aware of his capabilities, and knows that on his day, he can beat the best in the world. Would you like to make a difference Abhishek’s sporting journey? Would you like to lend a helping hand to Indian sports? Pledge your support to help him achieve glory for India!
