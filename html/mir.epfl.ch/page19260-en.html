            Team-ex

            MIR is a team of highly qualified and motivated researchers coming from various disciplines, but mostly from the social sciences. As a matter of principle, MIR's doctoral students are writing their thesis during a 4 year period while either working at MIR or in their professionnal environnement. MIR is directed by Professor Matthias Finger, assisted by Patricia Defferrard and Dr. Aris Christodoulou.

            PROFESSOR

            Prof. Dr. Dr. Matthias P. FINGER
            Full Professor
            CV in English

            Phone: +41 21 693 00 01
            matthias.finger@epfl.ch
            Office: Odyssea 303

             

            Born in 1955, Matthias Finger, a Swiss and French citizen, received his Ph.D. in Education in 1986 and his Ph.D. in Political Science in 1988, both from the University of Geneva. After having been Assistant Professor at Syracuse University, New York (1989-1991) and Associate Professor at Columbia University, New York (1992-1994), Matthias Finger was appointed Full Professor at the Swiss Graduate School of Public Administration in Lausanne in 1995. He was appointed Full Professor at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in October 2002. Between 2003 and 2009 he was the Dean of EPFL's School of Continuing Education. As of 2009, he is in charge of executive education at EPFL's Middle East campus. He is the co-editor in-chief of the Journal on Competition and Regulation in Network Industries since 2008. He has been appointed Area Director for Transport at the Florence School of Regulation in 2010. Matthias Finger's main interest is on the de- and re-regulation of the network industries (postal services, telecommunications, electricity, railways, air transport, urban public transport, water). An additional research focus is currently being developed on the intelligent governance of large urban systems. Matthias Finger is a member of the Swiss Federal Electricity Regulatory Comission (ELCOM) since 2007.

            RESEARCH ASSOCIATES

            Dr. Torben HOLVAD
            Research Associate

            Phone: +33 3 27 09 65 13
            torben.holvad@era.europa.eu
             

             

             

            Torben Holvad is an Economic Advisor at the European Railway Agency (ERA) in Valenciennes / Lille in France. His main responsibilities at ERA are the economic evaluation (cost-benefit analysis) of the recommendations being developed by the Agency. Prior to this position Torben Holvad was a Research Associate at the Transport Studies Unit at the University of Oxford and is maintaining academic links with Oxford University as senior research associate of the Oxford University Centre for the Environment (OUCE). He is also an external associate professor at the Department of Transport at the Danish Technical University. Torben Holvad has an MSc in economics from Copenhagen University and a PhD in economics from the European University Institute in Florence. He has significant expertise in transport economics with particular emphasis on regulation of public transport, transport infrastructure appraisal and regional economic development. He has been involved in many European funded research projects on transport since 1994. He has published numerous papers on efficiency and productivity measurement, appraisal of transport schemes, access pricing in the railway sector, computable general equilibrium models and transport policy issues.

            PhD Students

             

            Anne GREINUS

            PhD Candidate
             

            dagny.greinus@epfl.ch

            Anne Greinus obtained her diploma in transport economics at the Technical University of Dresden in 2004. After her studies, she worked as consultant for ProgTrans AG in Basel and as strategy expert for the division Infrastructure of the Swiss Federal Railways. Since 2009, she is economist for financing rail infrastructure in the Federal Office of Transport. Her PhD focuses on financing rail infrastructure and incentive regulation of rail infrastructure managers.

            Toni MÄNNISTÖ

            PhD Candidate

            toni.mannisto@epfl.ch

            Toni Männistö studied previously at Aalto University School of Science and Technology in Helsinki where he obtained his MSc degree in Industrial Engineering and Management in 2011. During his Bachelor and Master studies, Toni expanded his professional expertise by working for several companies including his current employer Cross-border Research Association. His project engagements dealt with themes like customs risk management, catastrophe logistics, tracking solutions in postal supply chains, and distribution of pharmaceuticals. His PhD focuses on security of global supply chains.

            PhD Candidate

            ralf.dyllick-brenzinger@epfl.ch

             

             

             

            Ralf M. Dyllick-Brenzinger is a PhD student at the Chair Management of Network Industries and conducts his research project under the supervision of Professor Finger. His research centers on technology aspects of energy policy in the Middle East and North Africa. Before joining EPFL in 2010, he worked for The Boston Consulting Group in its Munich and Dubai office. Ralf holds a "Lizenziat" (equivalent to a master's degree) in business administration from the University of St.Gallen, Switzerland.
