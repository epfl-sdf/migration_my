          Questions & Answers

           

          Presentation in Bern - Thursday, March 6, 2014

          Q&A Session

          The presentation given by Grégoire Bourban is available in PDF here.

           

          Attention: The A15 must be filled instead of the A10 in the PSS form. 

           

          • Are overheads included in the salaries?

          The hourly rates used for the financial proposal may include the overheads. ESA audited hourly rates can be used if available. For the academic partner, as the funding  will be granted by the SERI, the hourly rates may be similar to the ones used for CTI/KTI projects.

           

          • Is the PSS-form mandatory for all the partners?

          Yes, each partner must complete and submit a separate PSS form. One for the industrial and one for the academic partner.

           

          • Academic and industrial partners must send separated proposals?

          No, only one proposal shall be submitted for the team. The principal academic partner is the official prime contractor; he has the duty to manage the financial part of the project. The technical project manager can be from the industrial partner.

           

          • Who are the leader and the contact between SSO/SSC and the project?

          The academic partner fulfils the responsibilities linked to the financial and administrative elements. For all technical matters, the project manager is the point of contact for the SSC. All

           

          •  Are proposals of less than 250’000CHF are accepted?

          Yes.

           

          • If the granted funding covers salaries, who funds materials and hardware?

          Raw material and hardware manufacturing are to be covered additionally by the partners’ own funds.

           

          • Is VAT applicable to the funding?

          No. The payment is exempt of VAT since it is a subsidy which falls under public law contribution (Art.29c of the ordinance on Value Added Tax:

          http://www.admin.ch/opc/en/classified-compilation/20091866/index.html)

           

          • How many projects will be selected?

          Depending on the level of the proposals and based on previous years, around 10 projects are expected to be funded.

          • Is the evaluation process made public?

          The evaluation process is not publicly accessible, however feedback may be requested from the Swiss Space Center.

           

          • Who is the prime contractor and who shall submit the proposal?

          The academic partner is the prime contractor while the industrial partner is a sub-contractor. The prime contractor is responsible for submitting the proposal.

           

          • Are there priority areas of interest for the proposals?

          Yes, the SERI/SSO has indicated the priority areas of interest as described in the Swiss Space Implementation Plan (http://www.sbfi.admin.ch/themen/01371/01417/index.html?lang=de). Nonetheless, proposals outside these areas are welcome and will be considered in the evaluation process.

           

          •  Is there a limit to the number of proposals which can be submited by each entity?

          No, an entity can submit multiple projects with different or with the same partners.

           

          • What is the roadmap/strategy requested for in the proposal?

          The roadmap is the plan for future development of the subject of the proposal, for example, into a product, beyond the scope of the present funding being sought. What are the next steps foreseen for the hardware developed? Can the software developed being considered as a commercial product? These questions will be asked again during the final reviews.   

           

          • About the TRL levels of the technology proposed: is it one of the criteria for the selection?

          Usually, the targeted TRL are between 2-3 at the beginning and aiming to reach 4-partially 5 at the end. Basic research studies are not covered in the frame of this Call for Proposals as a technology transfer between the academic partner and the industry is foreseen.

           

          • At the end of the project, will the final product be required by the SSO or the SSC the final product? Must the Software or Hardware be made available?

          The final products (software or hardware) will remain the property of the applicants. The availability of the final product will depend on the agreement between the partners (a clear IP agreement will be required before the kick-off meeting).

           

          • If the project is successful, is there additional support for follow up to an ESA project?

          There is no follow-up funding foreseen in the frame of this Call for Proposals. The aim of this unique subsidy is to foster the competences of the participants in their fields of interest. However, the SER/SSO and the SSC can also be contacted at the end of the project to explore further possibilities of collaboration in ESA projects.

           

          • External ESA-ESTEC evaluation: geographical evaluation based on European competition is applied on the evaluation?

          No, the experts are free to comment on the matter, but European competition is not an evaluation criteria.

           

          •  Can Earth Application interest help in the evaluation?

          Yes, it can be underlined that a future application is linked to Earth application, however this cannot be the main area of interest since the MdP is based on Space Research and Technologies.

           

          •  Is it possible to have support from SSC or SSO to find a partner or is this information confidential?

          As the SSC is in charge of the implementation of this Call for Proposals which also includes the technical evaluation, the SSC does not play an active role in supporting potential applicants. The suggestion was brought forward to have a public section on the SSC webpage for current expression of interests where partners looking for a special competence can announce it.

           

          • Cooperation with foreign partners

          It is stated within the “Call for Proposals” terms: “In case of cooperation with a foreign partner being envisaged, it must be duly explained in the proposal, including a justification for why its proposed activity cannot be performed in Switzerland. Furthermore, its share may not exceed 10% of the funded amount.”
