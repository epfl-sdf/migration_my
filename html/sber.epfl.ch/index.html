SBER SBER
Streams and rivers sculpt the Earth surface, form dense drainage networks and perfuse the landscape like arteries. It is only recently that the relevance of stream ecosystems for global biogeochemical fluxes has been recognized. Within SBER, we are an interdisciplinary team intrigued by the beauty, ecology and biogeochemistry of stream ecosystems and the networks they form.

We are interested in biofilms, which are the dominant form of microbial life in streams. These biofilms are “jungles” where organisms from all three domains of life coexist. We study the assembly of these microbial “jungles” and the biodiversity dynamics therein ranging from the microbial scale to the scale of entire stream networks. Furthermore, we study stream ecosystem processes, such as whole-ecosystem metabolism, and related carbon fluxes. It is our endeavour to ultimately link biofilm ecology to theses ecosystem processes and ultimately to large-scale biogeochemical fluxes.

We blend environmental sciences, engineering and ecological theory, and combine experiments with fieldwork to gain mechanistic understanding of the coupled physical, chemical and biological processes (some call this “ecohydraulics”) that control microbial life and biogeochemistry in stream ecosystems.
