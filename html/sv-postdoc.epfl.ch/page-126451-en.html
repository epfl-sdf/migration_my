    Career Transitioning from Academia to Industry

    Pics

    Loading files... Please wait.

    7th October 2015 

    Organised by the HBA and the SV Postdoc Association

     

    Chair:              Caroline Lutz Lutz and Partners, Geneva

    Speakers:       Prof. Melanie Blokesch Tenure Track Professor in SV

    Toby Ernberg Executive Vice President at Coulter Partners

    Rolf Illum-Engsig Head of HR, NIHS

    Frederique Pedretti VP of International Quality, Edwards Lifesciences

    Verena Voelter Executive Director, Celgene Corp.

     

    After introductions from Nanci Govinder (HBA), Melanie Blokesch (EPFL), and Petra Schwalie (SV Postdoc Associaiton), a question and answer session was held.

     

    About our panel

    Verena started her career as an MD doing part time clinical research here in Lausanne, at the CHUV. From there, she moved into R&D at Celgene where she is now the commercial director.

     

    Coulter Partners works in the areas of medtech, biotech, and pharma, and is currently moving towards connected health. Coulter Partners is a British based company with an office in Nyon and another opening soon in Basel. Candidates are from all over the world. Toby previously worked at Reuters in Geneva.

     

    Rolf works in both academia and industry. Collaborators include CHUV and SV. NIHS hosts several masters and PhD students, as well as postdocs.

     

    Frederique worked for Novartis, in drug manufacturing, quality control, and as a senior global project manager, before moving to Edwards Lifesciences where she is now the vice president of international quality. Edwards Lifesciences works in the area of cardiovascular devices and is based in the US. Edwards employs engineers and scientists to push technologies. R&D is carried out in California and Israel, with manufacturing in the US and in Asia.

     

    Questions

    There were a range of questions and interesting responses, which are briefly summarised below.

     

    Q. What are the opportunities in your area?

    Verena:

    ·       Medical Affairs (between R&D and marketing): role involves discussing with doctors and scientists in the field. Celgene have hired PhDs, who have started as interns and progressed to managers.

    ·       If you have business sense: Start as a sales representative (entry through agencies.

    ·       Network!.... and know what’s out there.

     

    Rolf made an interesting point, stressing that you do not have to leave academia and join industry, but you can balance and integrate the two (and there are many examples of people that do this).

     

    Q. When searching for jobs in biotech, job specifications always ask for at least 3 years experience. How do you get around this?

    Verena:

    ·       Clinical trials units in hospitals to gain experience

    ·       Move comes with a “step backwards” career-wise - you will likely need to start as a clinical trials assistant or an intern to gain some experience.

    ·       Bigger companies might have more opportunities

    ·       Connect with a manager or director rather than HR

    ·       Recommendations are very important

    Frederique:

    ·       Start in a contract research organisation (CRO)

     

    Q. Is it easier to get into industry when you’re younger (i.e. straight after PhD)?

    Answers from all: You can’t generalise!

    Rolf: Passion and intent are what’s important.

    Verena: What you have specifically done is not necessarily important, but what you care about, and what you hope to achieve.

     

    Other points touched upon in the discussion:

     

    Take care of your network! (Rolf):

    ·       Keep conference participation lists

    ·       Keep in touch - send “goodies” e.g. interesting articles

    ·       75% of jobs are not visible to the public!!

    ·       Networking has to be natural - something that suits you

    ·       Identify people you want to speak to before you go to a meeting/conference

    ·       Join the HBA?

     

    Verena on networking:

    ·       Quality not quantity

    ·       People usually tend to stick to people they know, so set yourself a challenge e.g. meeting one new person at an event

     

    Frederique on networking:

    ·       Mentoring can be useful

     

    Carolyn on networking:

    ·       Every day

    ·       Always try to give something back

    ·       Make introductions

    ·       Join the HBA

     

    Report written by Nicola Dynes on behalf of the SV Postdoc Association
