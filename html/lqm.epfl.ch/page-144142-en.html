Student Project: Ultra low temperature magnetization, susceptibility and magnetocaloric measurements on LiMF4 samples

The LiMF4 compounds, where M is a rare-earth metal are realisations of anisotropic dipolar coupled model magnets. In the case of LiHoF4, the ground state is an Ising ferromagnet which undergoes a quantum phase transition when a magnetic field is applied perpendicular to the Ising axis (Ronnow et al., Science). When doped with Er, whose parent compound LiErF4 is an XY-antiferromagnet (Kraemer et al., Science) a complex phase diagram is obtained (Piatek et al., Phys Rev. B, see figure below). Interesting dynamics in LiHo0.50Er0.50F4 have been observed in simultateous measurements of magnetisation, AC susceptibility and magnetrocaloric effect when a field is applied parallel to the c-axis at temperatures below 100 mK. In this project we propose preparing, measuring and analysing samples with different Ho:Er ratios in order to better understand the origins of these dynamics.

 

 

Student project: Novel effective models based on spin-trimers

The low-energy physics of weakly interacting magnetic trimers exhibits strong frustration and anisotropic exchange interactions. Preliminary work shows that in the presence of a strong magnetic field, it may be accounted for by a quantum compass model. Exploratory work (exact diagonalization and analytical approaches) is needed to extend our understanding for this system for which physical realizations might exist.

Student Project: AC Magnetoelectric susceptibility measurements on chiral magnets

In magnetoelectric coupling systems, external electric field will affect their magnetic properties. If one applies an AC electric field via a lock-in amplifier and then measures the corresponding magnetic moment, they will get the AC magnetoelectric susceptibility dM/dE. Cu2OSeO3 is a magnetoelectric system with skyrmion lattice spin texture in a small range of the phase diagram. dM/dE measurement will give sharp features and hence sharp phase boundaries when experiencing phase transitions. This project would involve measuring new candidate samples in order to characterise their phase diagrams.

 

Student project: MC simulation of dipolar coupled model magnets

 

 

Monte Carlo simulation of LiErF4 and related compounds (see this article in Science). We had two student projects on this (reports Finco and Klughertz). It is promising in that simulations seems to match experimentally measured critical exponents. Validity of simulation needs to be investigated and systematic simmulations needs to be performed to lead to publishable results.

Student Project: Characterisation of ultra-low temperature thermometers

One of the most common thermometers used at ultra-low tempertures (< 1K) is the humble RuO2 resistor found in all modern electronics. These resistors which show minimal variations from their specified value around room temperature show a dramatic exponential increase in resistivity at low temperatures. This, coupled with their small size and plentiful availability makes them ideal temperature probes. Previous work done by P. Jorba involved designing and calibrating two batches of thermometers for use in the temperature range of 6 mK – 10K. The current project wold involve building on this base to determine which RT resistance values work best for different temperature ranges.

Student project: Systematic variational wavefunction optimization developpement

 

Models as simple as the spin-1/2 Heisenberg model on the square lattice lack an exact solution. While analytical approaches like spin-wave theory might be used to approximatly diagonalize the model, another route is to "guess" groundstate wavefunction candidates called variational wavefunctions. The evaluation of the energy of such variational wavefunction is in itself a computationally heavy task for which a software was developped in the lab. This project would consist of using this code and developp a systematic optimization procedure of the variational wavefunction parameters to minimize its variational energy.

Student Project: Software development and calibration of a dilution fridge SQUID magnetometer

 

A SQUID (Superconducting QUantum Interference Device) is probably the most sensitive instrument which can be used to measure magnetic properties. Although they exist commercially at ‘high’ temperature ranges of >2 K, they are incredibly rare at sub kelvin temperatures. One such magnetometer was developed by J O Piatek (PhD Thesis) at LQM and is capable of operating at temperatures < 100 mK. The project would involve integrating control software into the existing measurement software package and then using this software to fully calibrate the magnetometer.

Student project or job: Developpement and usage of in-house "Quantum Wolf" cluster

 

We offer the possibility to carry out various tasks related to our home-made cluster nick-named the "Quantum Wolf". These tasks range from carrying out various benchmarks, temperature monitoring and power consumption to using and monitoring sophiticated software on the cluster to calculate state-of-the-art pysical quantities like the dynamic structure factor of the 1D spin-1/2 chain.
