Health and Safety

Prevention is better than cure. Therefore, and in accordance with the "Directive concerning health and safety in the workplace" (DSST) [English] [Français], the Safety, Prevention and Health domain (DSPS) provides support and advice on occupational health and expertise in safeguarding employee's health at the work place. Please contact the Health Point for more information.
 

  • Health Point
  • Consultations with nurses
  • Occupational health


The “Ordinance on confined use” and the “Ordinance on the protection of workers exposed to microorganisms” ask that people professionally (collaborators, visitors, students) exposed to biological materials (samples from animal or human origin, potentially infectious or pathogenic microorganisms, viral vectors, primary cells, genetically modified organisms, etc.) must be medically supervised. In order to assess if an entry medical test is required, we ask all the collaborators of the SV faculty to fill the on-line questionnaire entitled “Questionnaire for exposure profile”. Special surveillance programs have also been established for people working with nanoparticles, specific hazardous chemicals (e.g. arsenic), etc.
  

  • Questionnaire for exposure profile [English] [Français]
  • Working with nano-objects
     

In addition to the health and safety at work on the campus, the occupational health also provides services in relation to your professional travels.
 

  • Information for EPFL staff & students going abroad
  • Vaccination & travel medicine

 

The “Ordinance on the protection of maternity” sets the principal measures that govern health protection at work during pregnancy. Pregnant women are warmly encouraged to contact The “Health point” in order to get advice or a professional evaluation of their work place.
