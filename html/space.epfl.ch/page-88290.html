          Next Generation CubeSat: CubETH

          CubETH systems engineering

          Types: Master
          Responsibles: Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Electrical, Micro and Mechanical Engineering
          Keywords: Space, system engineering, CubeSat, CubETH

          CubETH is a CubeSat (10x10x10 cm3) satellite under development between ETHZ and EPFL. Satellite science goal is Precise Orbit Determination (POD) and satellite attitude determination based on GNSS (signals from GPS and GLONASS) . The phase B is currently in progress since April 2013. Students from ALL sections are invited to accomplish tasks such as mission design, systems engineering.

          Work will be perofmed in the EPFL Space Center Concurrent Design Facility. Main task is to maintain the Interface Control Document and understand details of all satellite subsystems. This work will also contribute to writing of requirements, scheduling of tasks in the project and keeping mass/volume, power, data and costs budget.

          Telecommunication Subsystem

          CubETH Communication board software design and forward error correction implementation

          Types: Semester / Master
          Responsible:           Federico Belloni, Anton Ivanov

          Categories: Communication, Embedded Systems

          Keywords: Flight software, FEC, Viterbi, BCH,  Reed-solomon, ECSS, CCSDS, CubeSat, CubETH

          CubETH is a CubeSat (10x10x10 cm3) satellite under development between ETHZ and EPFL. The satellite will perform precise orbit determination and satellite attitude determination based on GNSS in post-processing and in real-time. The project is at moment in phase B and will pass on phase C at beggning of 2014. A preliminary design of the communication board is available however the existing software (based on Swisscube) needs to be updated. The work will consist on design the driver for the communication between microcontroller and transceivers IC and to implement FEC to increase link robustness.

          CubETH simulation and characterization of UHF/VHF antennas

          Types: Semester
          Responsibles: Federico Belloni,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Communication, RF
          Keywords: Antennas, UHF, VHF, CubeSat, CubETH
           

          CubETH is a CubeSat (10x10x10 cm3) satellite under development between ETHZ and EPFL. The satellite will perform precise orbit determination and satellite attitude determination based on GNSS in post-processing and in real-time. The project is at moment in phase B and will pass on phase C at begging of 2014. The work consists on the simulation of the new antenna system for CubETH and his characterization.
           

          CubETH COM board flight model design

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Federico Belloni,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: RF electronics
          Keywords: RF, UHF, VHF, CubeSat, CubETH


          A preliminary design of the communication board is available however the board need to be optimized (impedance matching) and redesign to move from a development board to flight hardware. The work will consist on optimize current design, design the flight hardware and test it.

           

          CubETH Command and data management

          CubETH Internal Communication

          Types: Semester / Master
          Responsibles: Louis Masson, Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Embedded Systems
          Keywords: Space, CDMS, CubeSat

          This project will contribute to standardisation of CubETH's internal communication protocol and implementation of the communication protocol in all of the subsystems. The student will work on the CubETH Flat Sat and harmonise the communication between all the subsystems, which will be a first step in the unification of the flight software of the satellite bus. The student will also need to finalise the ground segment to satellite communication by reviewing the TM / TC packet dictionary and implement the missing services.

          Attitude Determination and Control Subsystem Software Design

          Types: Semester / Master
          Responsibles: Louis Masson, Stefano Rossi, Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Embedded Systems          , Attitude Control
          Keywords: Space, CDMS, CubeSat

          This project will contribute to the implmenetation of the flight software for the CubETH satellite. The following aspects will be considered in this workpackage: 

          • Implement the SPI communication between the CDMS and ADCS
          • Write drivers for the magnetorquers and sun sensors
          • Implement and optimise the ADCS algorithms on both CDMS and ADCS (task distribution between the two MCUs will be determined in advance by analysing the computational requirements of the different ADCS algorithms).
          • Evaluate CPU overhead left for the CDMS after implementation of the ADCS.

          Good matchematical background and embedded system development interest is required.

          Energy and Power Subsystem

          CubETH battery board flight model design
           

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Reto Wiesendanger, Federico Belloni,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanics, electronics
          Keywords: Battery, Thermal control, PCB design, Mechanics design, CubeSat, CubETH

          Based on Swisscube design the student will have to design the new hermetic battery box and design the new PCB with the batteries control and thermal management.

          Categories: Electronics

          The aim of the project is to test and validate the actual prototype and design the flight version of the electrical power board.
           

          Attitude Determination and Control Subsystem

          CubETH Hardware in the Loop (HIL) Simulator:
           

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Stefano Rossi,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical, Embedded system development, Data analysis
          Keywords: embedded system, mechanical prototyping, microtechnique, robotics

          Number of positions available: 3

          HIL is the first validation of the ADCS software mounted on board. In order to start the validation a test set-up is necessary. The aim of the project is to design and prepare a three degrees of freedom environment useful to test the ADCS of the satellite and the entire satellite, simulating the space environment in all its characteristics. The test set-up should connect the ADCS (or the spacecraft itself) to the computers that needs to manage both the ADCS and the environment set up.

          Required competences: mechanical competences (CAD), microelectronics-electronics (connections with boards, helmoltz-coils, wires etc)

          CubETH Sun Sensors

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Stefano Rossi,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical, Embedded system development, Data analysis
          Keywords: embedded systems, mechanical prototyping, microtechnique, robotics

          The Sun Sensors are the "eyes" of a satellite: the goal of this project is to design, fabricate and tests the sun sensors in order to complete the attitude determination of CubETH. The sun sensors are sensible detectors that detect the Sun-vector useful for the Determination algorithms. The task is to design and calibrate a small but precise sensor with its interfaces to fulfill the determination requirements for the mission.
           

          Required competences: microelectronics-electronics
           

          CubETH Sensors implementation and calibration.

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Stefano Rossi,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical, Embedded system development, Data analysis
          Keywords: embedded systems, mechanical prototyping, microtechnique, robotics

          The aim of this project is to conclude the sensor's characterization and to implement the characteristics into the algorithm of the attitude control. The goal is to code in C the functions and the characteristics found into the main Attitude Control Loop in order to predict the behavior of the sensors and preventing drift, biases and high level of noises.
           

          Required competences: microelectronics-electronics, C code

          CubETH algorithm implementation and validation (2).

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Stefano Rossi,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical, Embedded system development, Data analysis
          Keywords: embedded systems, mechanical prototyping, microtechnique, robotics

          Since part of the main algorithm of control has been coded in C and started the implementation on the microcontroller on board the satellite, the next step will be to complete the implementation and the migration from Matlab to C of the remaining algorithm's parts such as: orbit propagator, magnetic field model prediction, extended Kalman filter etc. The final goal is to validate and mount it on board.

          Required competences: C code, (microelectronics-electronics)

          CubETH b-dot algorithm and validation.

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Stefano Rossi,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical, Embedded system development, Data analysis
          Keywords: embedded systems, mechanical prototyping, microtechnique, robotics

          The main algorithm for the attitude control wants to align the body with a certain face to the nadir axis, however in case of failure the ADCS needs to guarantee a certain requirement of stability: in this project the main goal is to re-implemented the tested and validated algorithm flown with Swisscube and adapt it to CubETH and its hardware.

          CubETH Mechanical and Structures

          Spacecraft configuration and integration (TBD)

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Reto Wiesendanger,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical
          Keywords: CAD, Mechanical, prototyping, manufacturing. 

          The optimal placement of the sub systems inside the spacecraft is crucial for the reliability, structural and electrical performance of the overall system. The student will have to understand all interactions and connections between each sub systems, sensors, actuators , electrical components and structural elements. As well the student will have to define a strategy for assembly of the spacecraft.

           

          Thermal Design (TBD)

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Reto Wiesendanger,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical and Thermal analysis
          Keywords: CAD, Heat transfer, Thermal analysis


          The thermal sub-system of the spacecraft needs to keep the temperature of all components inside nominal operational temperatures. This could be by either cooling them with heat sinks or heat them with electrical heaters. The student will have to create a thermal model of the satellite using ANSYS software, assess the temperatures observed and optimize the thermal design.

           

          Antenna deployment system

          Types: Semester/Master
          Responsibles: Reto Wiesendanger,           Anton Ivanov
          Categories: Mechanical
          Keywords: CAD, Mechanical, prototyping, manufacturing. 

          The space Center has developed a prototype of a deployment mechanism for the communication antennas. The prototype needs improvments in terms of mass, size and reliability. Mechanical design work needs to be performed to fit the prototype in the mass and volume constraints.
