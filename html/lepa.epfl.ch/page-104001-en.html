    People

    LEPA

    Prof. Hubert Girault
    Head of the Laboratory
     
    Prof. Girault established LEPA at EPFL in 1992. In the 20 years he has authored of 2 textbooks, is co-author of more than 400 scientific publications  and the co-inventor of more than 15 patents. His academic interests range from the fundamental to applied, and he is internationally recognised as a leader in the filed electrochemistry. Further information can be found here.
     
    Ms. Véronique Amstutz
    Final year PhD student
     
    Véronique has been the primary researcher on the dual-circuit RFB and is funded by EOS Holding under the project "Making Hydrogen: Electrochemical Energy Storage". Véronique is now in her final year with the PhD project reaching full completion with all objective attained, if not surpassed. Alongside this project, she is presently undertaking fundamental RFB research in alternative directions.
     
    Dr. Heron Vrubel
    Posdoctoral Researcher/ Site manager
     
    Heron received his PhD from EPFL in July 2013. He was a student in Prof. Hu's group and was focused on the synthesis and characterisation of novel hydrogen evolution catalysts based on molybdenum compounds. His research has been published in a number of high impact journals, and the low cost catalysts developed are fundamental to the dual-circuit RFB research being developed here. Heron is a very capable engineer, with an exceptional knowledge of laboratory equipment and and chemical engineering systems. He is also an excellent materials chemist.

     

    Dr. Pekka Peljo
    Postdoctoral researcher
     
    Pekka recently joined LEPA in July 2013 as a postdoc following the completion of his thesis at Aalto University, Finland. Pekka has a background in electrochemistry, particularly in the field of liquid-liquid systems. He also has a good knowlegde of applied electrochemical systems and chemical engineering. Pekka has exisiting knowledge of RFBs and novel circuitry from his previous university group, with a 2013 publication in PCCP regarding a thermally regenerative all-copper RFB.
     
    Former team members:
     
    Dr Kathryn Toghill
    Postdoctoral researcher
     
    Kathryn joined LEPA in October 2011 following the completion of her DPhil thesis in electrochemistry at Oxford University. She has held a leading role in the development of the dual-circuit RFB project since her arrival. Her previous research was primarily in electroanalytical and catalytic chemistry, but her more recent academic interests lie in energy conversion, production and storage by electrochemical means. Kathryn will start as a lecturer in chemistry at University of Lancaster, UK, in November 2014.
