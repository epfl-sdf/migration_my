    Job-search outside the university for Life Science Postdocs and PhDs

    Working in a laboratory, apart from a technical expertise in executing experiments, involves other tasks necessary for proper project management. Therefore, during our PhD thesis we acquire many non-scientific skills that are even further developed during our postdoctoral work. These often involve the capability to work in team, interdisciplinary collaborations, supervision of students or technicians, data presentation to a wide public and many others. Strikingly, all these processes are often, if not always, essential in other working environments as industry or non-profit companies. Identification and proper presentation of these skills might be helpful in a future job search outside the university.

    A workshop “Job-search outside the university for a Postdoc and PhD in Life Sciences” addressed a set of questions considering finding a career outside the academic environment for a qualified scientist as Postdocs or PhD students.  The workshop was conducted by Dr Verity Elston, a director of the CUSO (Conference  Universitaire de Suisse Occidentale) transferable skills program. 

    The 1-day workshop that took place on the 24th of May 2014, 15 Postdocs and PhD student could:

    ·       identify their own generic and transferable skills and learn how to present them to the future employer

    ·       understand better the types of employment that fits their skills and interest

    ·       understand better how to find relevant job ads in the public and private sector (how to read ads and compare with their professional profile)

    ·       receive general advice on how to write a CV and cover letter

    ·       learn how to develop a professional network

    ·       learn what is important during the job interview

    ·       have a better knowledge of potential employers for Life Sciences in Switzerland

    After the workshop, 12 participants were asked to fill in a survey that would help in content determination of future workshops.
